About SkyCast

At SkyCast, our mission is simple: to make weather data clear, accessible, and useful for everyone. From casual users wanting to know if they need an umbrella, to professionals relying on hourly forecasts, we aim to provide information that helps people plan their day with confidence.

Our Story

SkyCast began as a student project, designed to combine the power of modern web technologies with real-world data from the OpenWeatherMap API. What started as a small experiment quickly grew into a polished platform with live weather updates, a 5-day forecast, an hourly forecast, and an interactive weather map.

Along the way, we placed a strong emphasis on accessibility, ensuring that our service is inclusive for visually impaired users through features like high-contrast mode and screen-reader support.

Our Technology

SkyCast is built using clean and modern web standards: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Live weather data is powered by OpenWeatherMap, while interactive mapping is delivered through Leaflet.js and OpenStreetMap. The design features responsive layouts, an autumn-inspired theme, and card-based components that adapt across desktops, tablets, and smartphones.

Our Commitment

We believe in building tools that are not only functional but also enjoyable to use. SkyCast is continuously improved, with future enhancements planned such as saving favorite cities, adding multi-language support, and generating trend charts for temperature, wind, and humidity.
